Project overview

One visit, arranged as a living catalogue

The exhibit is organized across seven chapters — gallery rooms, transitional spaces, and an immersive installation — each drawn from works and spaces encountered during the visit. Natural history paintings, a pre-colonial skull, archival photographs, literary portraits, and Clyde Aedriel Rafael's Dáyat installation are presented here as a single curated sequence.

Soft light and layered texture let each room read like its own small installation.

Curatorial concept

Seven chapters, one continuous visit

The exhibit is arranged across seven distinct spaces — from gallery rooms dense with painted works and heritage artifacts, through open transitional areas, and into the immersive blue installation that closes the sequence.

01–04

The Gallery Rooms

Galleries I through IV move from a natural history wall and heritage portraits, through pre-colonial artifacts, to the Dáyat installation — a submerged blue room of rattan waves and suspended fish built from the coastal memory of Pangasinan.

05–06

The Passage & The Atrium

Between the rooms, the museum opens up. These transitional spaces — corridor and atrium — carry their own quiet attention. What people notice when they are not looking for anything in particular is often the most honest record of where they were.

07

The Wing

The final chapter is the loosest. Poses are less composed, the attention more diffuse — and in that looseness, the photographs become more true. The exhibit does not end cleanly. It lingers in the people moving through its last rooms.

Project overview

Curated as an interactive museum exhibit

The exhibit is organized around selected works and landmark spaces documented during the visit. It turns observation, interpretation, and visual research into an interactive format that supports curation, art analysis, and digital presentation.

Soft light, layered texture, and the quiet pulse of a gallery full of friends.

Exhibit curation

Five significant works from the visit

Each selected work opens into a short image-led fragment drawn from the atmosphere of the room.

Curatorial concept

A sequence shaped by environment, heritage, and immersion

The exhibit is arranged to move from observed wall-based works, to heritage-inspired spaces, and finally to immersive light installations that change how the viewer reads form and atmosphere.

01

Green Wall Gallery

The first room centers on framed studies and painted surfaces, introducing a theme of observation and placing the viewers directly within the display of art.

02

Ruins And Sunset Sets

The middle sequence brings in ruin-like architecture and projected scenery, connecting the exhibit to historical atmosphere, built heritage, and theatrical presentation.

03

Blue Ocean Installation

The closing rooms rely on luminous blue installation work, where atmosphere, reflection, and movement become the final emotional focus of the exhibit.
